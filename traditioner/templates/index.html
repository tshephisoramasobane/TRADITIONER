The Bapedi tribe

The Bapedi tribe arose from small chiefdoms that were formed before the 17th century. They were then defeated in the 19th century by the armies of Mzilikazi the king who founded the Matebele kingdom. They were then revived by the Pedi chief Sekweti. Today if you want to meet the Bapedi people you need to travel to the Northern part of South Africa in a province called Limpopo.


The Bapedi language

The languge spoken by the Bapedi people is known as Sepedi. It is usually mistaken to be the same as Northern Sotho because of them sounding the same. The confusin about the two languages came about as a result of the fact that missionaries who developed the orthography for Northern Sotho mostly had contact with the Pedi people. These two are different languages.


The Bapedi food

Bapedi eat meat and vegetables and dishes like thophi made from maize meal and a vegetable called lerotse that looks like a watermelon from the outside but it is yellowish inside and it tastes sweeter when it is cooked. They also like Mashotja known as Mopani worms. Their favorite vegetable is Lerotho a green vegetable with white flowers that grows easily even in their backyards. The food is cooked on the ground using firewood and a three-legged pot. In the ancient times there was no electricity or gas to cook. Even today there are people who are still following the Bapedi culture prefer using firewood and three-legged pots using firewood.


Tlhotlwa

The Bapedi people's traditional beer is called Tlhotlwa. It is normally drank by the elderly men when they are garthered in in a place called Kgoro which is a gathering place for the village elderly men and the chiefs.It is also served at traditional ritual ceremonies,weddings and other celebrations. It is made of Sorghum and the process of making this sorghum beer from cooking it and brewing it takes about a week. It is only prepared by the elderly women of the village and they will then take it to the elderly men to taste and approve it. The drink is served to guests in clay pots, remember it is a strong beer and they mostly end up being drunk.


Mmino wa setxo(Traditional dance)

The people create music by playing aluminium pipes of different heights to reproduce vocal harmonies. Women dance on their knees, usually accompanied by drums, a lead singer and backup vocals. The Pedi people have the following types of cultural music:

  • Mpepetlwane: music played by young girls.
  • Mmatxhidi: it is played by older men and women.
  • Dipela: it is music played by everyone.
  • Dinaka/Kiba: music only played by men and boys.
